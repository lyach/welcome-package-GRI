Introduction

This guide is here to help you settle in smoothly and make the most of your Mitacs Globalink Research Internship at the University of Toronto. Inside, you'll find key information about arriving in the city, getting around campus, accessing food and services.

Airport

Nearest International Airport

Toronto Pearson International Airport (YYZ)

Transportation Services at the Airport

  • Taxis: Available at designated taxi stands on the arrivals level of each terminal.
  • Ride-Sharing (Uber, Lyft): Pick-up locations are:
    • Terminal 1: Ground Level, Door Q
    • Terminal 3: Arrivals Level, Door D
  • Public Transit: The UP Express train connects Pearson Airport to Union Station in downtown Toronto in about 25 minutes.

Important Notes

  • Request a Visitor Record before exiting immigration. Mitacs strongly encourages interns to request a Visitor Record (IMM 1097) when entering Canada as this can be used as identification alongside your passport when opening a bank account in Canada. This is at the discretion of the immigration officer.
  • Currency: Have at least $500 CAD in cash upon arrival for immediate expenses.
  • Documents: Carry all essential documents, including your passport, visa, and internship offer letter.

Next Immediate Steps

  1. Get a SIM card – if your phone plan is not international. Freedom and Fido offer affordable plans.
  2. Open a bank account – it's preferable you do this after you have a working SIM to link your account to.
  3. Request a Social Insurance Number (SIN) & upload to the GRI portal – it will take some days to receive in the mail.
    4. SIN Application

Bank Account

Choose a Bank

Consider major banks like TD Canada Trust, Scotiabank, or BMO. It's advisable to book an appointment online in advance, though walk-ins are typically accepted.

Required Documents

Bring the following to your appointment:

  • Visitor record
  • Passport
  • Internship offer letter
  • Cash (if applicable)

Student Banking Plans

Inquire about student accounts, which often offer:

  • No monthly fees
  • Unlimited transactions
  • Easy account closure before leaving Canada

University Access

Student ID (TCard)

Ensure you obtain your University of Toronto student ID as soon as possible, as it grants access to various campus facilities (libraries and gyms).

Libraries

The University of Toronto Libraries system is the largest academic library in Canada. Be sure to make the most out of their resources and beautiful buildings to study in!

Libraries Website

Transportation

  • Public Transportation: The Toronto Transit Commission (TTC) operates buses, streetcars, and subways throughout the city.
  • Ride apps: Uber and Lyft.
  • Walking: Toronto is renowned for its walkability, especially in neighborhoods like the Bay Street Corridor, Church-Yonge Corridor, and Kensington-Chinatown.

Extra Advice

  • Packing: Bring layers, as Toronto's weather can be unpredictable. A light jacket and umbrella are recommended.
  • Electronics: Canada uses Type A and B electrical outlets with a standard voltage of 120V.

About the City

  • Weather: In June and July, Toronto has average highs of 22°C to 25°C and lows of 10°C to 17°C.
  • Indigenous Land Acknowledgment: Toronto is situated on the traditional territory of the Mississaugas of the Credit, the Anishnabeg, the Chippewa, the Haudenosaunee, and the Wendat peoples.
  • Cultural Diversity: Toronto is one of the most multicultural cities in the world, with over 200 ethnic origins represented.

Toronto Bucket List

Visit the CN Tower

One of the tallest freestanding structures in the world.

Explore Kensington Market

A vibrant and diverse neighborhood known for its eclectic shops, cafes, and street art.

Stroll Through High Park

Toronto's largest public park offers beautiful trails, gardens, and a zoo.

Relax on the Toronto Islands

Take a ferry to these car-free islands offering beaches, picnic spots, and stunning skyline views.

Visit the Art Gallery of Ontario (AGO)

Admire a vast collection of artworks, including Canadian, Indigenous, and contemporary pieces. It's free for visitors under 25.

Discover the Royal Ontario Museum (ROM)

Canada's largest museum, housing over 18 million artifacts!

Visit Casa Loma

Tour Toronto's only castle, featuring elegant rooms, secret passages, and beautiful gardens.

Shop and Dine at St. Lawrence Market

One of the world's great markets, offering fresh produce, meats, and artisanal goods.
